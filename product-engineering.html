CATIA-Based Product Engineering

Product Engineering Workflow

Our structured workflow develops automotive concepts into production-ready CAD models, assemblies, and engineering documentation.

Stage 01

Requirement Analysis

Define functional requirements, vehicle packaging constraints, interfaces, and target engineering conditions before CAD development begins.

  • ✔ Boundary Definition
  • ✔ Packaging Studies
  • ✔ Space Allocation
Stage 02

Concept Design

Develop feasible mechanical concepts, package studies, and initial layouts aligned with styling surfaces and vehicle interfaces.

  • ✔ Concept Feasibility
  • ✔ Alternative Layouts
  • ✔ Section Proposals
Stage 03

3D CAD Modeling (CATIA)

Create high-precision parametric CATIA models for BIW structures, sheet metal parts, plastic trim, and automotive assemblies.

  • ✔ Class-A Surface Feasibility
  • ✔ Parametric Part Modeling
  • ✔ Component CAD Design
Stage 04

Detailed Engineering

Refine production-ready CAD models with feature definition, joining interfaces, tolerance requirements, and manufacturing feasibility considerations.

  • ✔ Wall Thickness definition
  • ✔ Draft Angle Analysis
  • ✔ Plastic Clips & Bosses
Stage 05

2D Engineering Drawings

Prepare detailed part and assembly engineering drawings with dimensions, GD&T, notes, and release information.

  • ✔ General Arrangement Drawings
  • ✔ Manufacturing Drawings
  • ✔ Production Drawings
Stage 06

Engineering Change Management

Assess engineering change requests, update CATIA models and drawings, and maintain controlled revision data.

  • ✔ Change Impact Analysis
  • ✔ Revision Updates
Stage 07

Final CAD Data Release

Complete CAD and drawing checks, consolidate release packages, and deliver production-ready engineering data to the customer.

  • ✔ Assembly Structure Check
  • ✔ Production-Ready Releases
  • ✔ Design Compliance Check
